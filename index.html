Unjust LAW?

Unjust laws, laws that defy a right to a human’s nature. It is our responsability to rebel against and break unjust laws with certain methods. Non-violent protests provide a safer more efficient gateway to protesting laws oppressing the people. In the process you create an image of your cause and set an example of how to deal with future injustices.

"NON"-violent Protest

Many countries and groups that are targeted and exploited often resort to violence which leads to further damages on both ends of the conflict. To follow the stance of violence will not solve more violence, we choose an alternative method that is just as effective. Peaceful protests have been shown to be not only effective, but symbolically powerful and uplifting for oppressed communities and people.. Key methods people have used is to hold flags/colors as a community, paint as protest, and work to the fullest extent to create economic shutdown. Methods such as displaying flags and colors which represents the unity of a population allows for the visible representation of those oppressed. Painting as protest gives way to forms of expression for the people and by the people and an economic shutdown forcibly puts the government in a position that cannot be ignored. These protests put the government's actions in the light of the public to bring awareness and unrest. Flags/Colors

Flags and colors have been used throughout history to bring awareness to a specific populus. In well known parades and visibility gatherings flags and colors have been used as a major factor. The LGBTQIAA+ community, ethnicity parades, and protest movements all carry banners or flags to represent the people and their needs. Many equality laws are dismissed due to the lack of visibility for the groups and people who need liberation from unjust laws. Without the exposure of the public to these groups through showings, many go unnoticed,

Paint as protest

Paint as protest is a symbolically powerful alternative to violence. It displays public unrest and uprising in a visually stunning and uplifting manner. An example of paint as protest being used is in Macedonia. Thousands of people rose together to fire paint filled balloons at the main government buildings and police. They demanded a stop to the government's violations of privacy by wire tapping. The president was then enveloped by the pressure of his people and rescinded pardons and called off pardons for those involved in the wire-tapping scandal.

Economic Shutdown

Economic shutdown is the ultimate threat to a government’s and leader’s power. The government cannot function without funds or nationwide activity. A show of how this has been done are the protests of Zimbabwe and Guatemala. In Zimbabwe the people have taken to a two day national shutdown in order to pressure the government to change unjust laws and to atone for the mismanagement of the country. In Guatemala, and economic shutdown was used to overthrow Jorge Ubico, a ruthless dictator. The dictator's power disintegrated under the pressure and he resigned shortly after.
